# Salary Negotiation and Compensation Reference

## Compensation Components

### Total Compensation Breakdown
| Component | Description | Typical % of Total |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| Base Salary | Fixed annual pay | 60-80% |
| Annual Bonus | Performance-based cash | 5-30% |
| Equity/RSUs | Stock grants vesting over time | 0-40% |
| Sign-on Bonus | One-time joining bonus | One-time |
| Benefits | Health, 401k match, etc. | 15-25% value add |

### Equity Valuation
- **RSUs**: Number of shares x current stock price
- **Options**: (Current price - Strike price) x Number of shares
- **Private company**: Use last valuation or 409A, apply 30-50% illiquidity discount
- **Vesting**: Typically 4 years with 1-year cliff; calculate annual value

## Negotiation Framework

### Phase 1: Research (Before Negotiation)
1. Gather market data from multiple sources:
   - Levels.fyi (tech), Glassdoor, Payscale, LinkedIn Salary
   - H-1B salary database (public companies)
   - Blind (anonymous verified compensation)
   - Robert Half Salary Guide, Hays Salary Guide (by industry)
2. Identify your target range: aim for 75th-90th percentile if justified
3. Calculate your walk-away number (minimum acceptable)

### Phase 2: Timing
- **Best time to negotiate**: After receiving a written offer, before accepting
- **Never negotiate**: During initial screening or before an offer is extended
- **Express enthusiasm first**: Always show genuine interest before discussing numbers

### Phase 3: The Negotiation Conversation

#### Opening (After Receiving Offer)
> "Thank you so much for the offer — I'm really excited about this role and the team. I've done some market research, and based on my [X years of experience / specific skills / track record of Y], I was hoping we could discuss the compensation to better align with my expectations and the market rate."

#### Stating Your Counter
> "Based on my research and the value I'd bring, I was targeting a base salary in the range of $[X] to $[Y]. Is there flexibility to move closer to that range?"

#### If They Push Back
> "I understand there may be constraints on base salary. Could we explore other components — such as a sign-on bonus, additional equity, or an accelerated review cycle?"

#### If They Say It's Final
> "I appreciate your transparency. Could I have [2-3 days / until end of week] to consider the full package?"

### Phase 4: Evaluating the Full Package
Score each component on a 1-5 scale:

| Factor | Weight | Score | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|-------|
| Base salary | 25% | | vs. market rate |
| Bonus/commission | 15% | | realistic target |
| Equity | 15% | | vesting, growth potential |
| Benefits | 15% | | health, 401k, PTO |
| Growth opportunity | 15% | | title, promotions, learning |
| Culture/flexibility | 15% | | remote, hours, team |

Weighted score > 3.5 = strong offer; < 2.5 = consider declining

## Negotiation Rules

1. **Never give your number first** if you can avoid it
2. **Always negotiate from a written offer** — verbal offers can change
3. **Negotiate all components**, not just base salary
4. **Get everything in writing** before accepting
5. **Never bluff about competing offers** you don't have
6. **Be collaborative, not adversarial** — "I want to make this work"
7. **Know when to stop** — pushing too hard damages the relationship
8. **Express gratitude** regardless of outcome

## Common Negotiation Scenarios

### Competing Offers
> "I have another offer at $[X], but your company is my top choice. Is there room to close the gap on compensation?"

### Internal Promotion/Raise
> "Over the past year, I've [specific achievements with metrics]. Based on my expanded scope and market data for this level, I'd like to discuss adjusting my compensation to $[X]."

### Startup Equity
- Ask: What's the total share count? (to calculate ownership %)
- Ask: What's the latest 409A valuation?
- Ask: What's the vesting schedule and acceleration on change of control?
- Ask: What's the exercise window post-departure?

### Counter-Offer from Current Employer
- 50-80% of people who accept counter-offers leave within 18 months
- Evaluate: Were you valued before threatening to leave?
- Consider: Will this change the underlying issues that made you look?

## Salary Ranges by Level (US Tech, 2024-2025 Reference)

These are approximate total compensation ranges for major tech markets:

| Level | Years Exp | Base Range | Total Comp Range |
|-------|-----------|------------|-----------------|
| Junior/Entry (L3) | 0-2 | $80-130K | $100-180K |
| Mid (L4) | 2-5 | $120-180K | $160-280K |
| Senior (L5) | 5-10 | $160-230K | $250-450K |
| Staff (L6) | 8-15 | $200-300K | $400-700K |
| Principal (L7) | 12+ | $250-400K | $600K-1.2M |

*Ranges vary significantly by company, location, and specialization.*
