# Interview Preparation Reference

## STAR Method Framework

Every behavioral answer should follow:
- **S**ituation: Brief context (1-2 sentences)
- **T**ask: Your specific responsibility
- **A**ction: Steps YOU took (use "I", not "we")
- **R**esult: Quantified outcome + what you learned

## Top 30 Behavioral Questions by Category

### Leadership & Influence
1. Tell me about a time you led a team through a difficult challenge
2. Describe a situation where you had to influence without authority
3. How have you handled conflict between team members?
4. Tell me about a time you had to make an unpopular decision
5. Describe when you mentored someone successfully

### Problem-Solving & Initiative
6. Tell me about a complex problem you solved at work
7. Describe a time you identified a problem before others noticed
8. How did you handle a situation with incomplete information?
9. Tell me about a time you improved a process
10. Describe a creative solution you developed

### Teamwork & Collaboration
11. Tell me about a successful cross-functional project
12. How do you handle disagreements with colleagues?
13. Describe a time you had to work with a difficult person
14. Tell me about a time you helped a struggling teammate
15. How have you built consensus on a controversial idea?

### Adaptability & Resilience
16. Tell me about a time you had to adapt to major change
17. Describe your biggest professional failure and what you learned
18. How did you handle a project that went off track?
19. Tell me about a time you received tough feedback
20. Describe when you had to learn something quickly

### Communication
21. Tell me about presenting complex information to non-experts
22. How have you delivered difficult news to stakeholders?
23. Describe a time your communication prevented a problem
24. Tell me about handling a miscommunication
25. How have you persuaded someone to change their mind?

### Results & Achievement
26. What's your greatest professional achievement?
27. Tell me about exceeding expectations on a project
28. How do you prioritize competing deadlines?
29. Describe a time you delivered results under pressure
30. Tell me about a goal you set and achieved

## Technical Interview Frameworks

### System Design (Software Engineering)
1. **Clarify requirements**: Functional and non-functional
2. **Estimate scale**: Users, QPS, storage
3. **High-level design**: Components and data flow
4. **Deep dive**: Database schema, API design, algorithms
5. **Address bottlenecks**: Scaling, caching, monitoring

### Case Interview (Consulting/Business)
1. **Clarify the problem**: Restate and confirm
2. **Structure**: Apply framework (profitability, market entry, M&A)
3. **Analyze**: Work through each branch with data
4. **Synthesize**: Summarize findings and recommendation
5. **Next steps**: Implementation considerations

### Product Interview (Product Management)
1. **Clarify**: User, problem, success metrics
2. **User segments**: Identify and prioritize
3. **Pain points**: Map user journey and friction
4. **Solutions**: Brainstorm, prioritize by impact/effort
5. **Metrics**: Define success measurement
6. **Tradeoffs**: Discuss risks and alternatives

## Questions to Ask the Interviewer

### About the Role
- What does success look like in the first 90 days?
- What are the biggest challenges facing this role?
- How is performance measured?

### About the Team
- Can you describe the team structure and dynamics?
- How does the team handle disagreements?
- What's the team's biggest priority this quarter?

### About the Company
- What are the company's top strategic priorities?
- How would you describe the company culture?
- What do you enjoy most about working here?

### Red Flag Signals to Watch For
- Vague answers about role expectations
- High turnover in the position
- Inability to describe success metrics
- Negative comments about current/former employees
- Pressure to accept quickly

## Interview Day Checklist

### Before
- [ ] Research company: mission, recent news, competitors, financials
- [ ] Review job description and map your experience to requirements
- [ ] Prepare 8-10 STAR stories covering all behavioral categories
- [ ] Practice answers aloud (record yourself)
- [ ] Prepare 5+ questions for interviewers
- [ ] Confirm logistics: time, location/link, interviewer names
- [ ] Test technology for virtual interviews

### During
- [ ] Arrive 10-15 minutes early
- [ ] Bring copies of resume, notepad, pen
- [ ] Listen fully before answering
- [ ] Use STAR format for behavioral questions
- [ ] Ask thoughtful questions at the end
- [ ] Get interviewer contact information

### After
- [ ] Send thank-you emails within 24 hours
- [ ] Reference specific conversation points
- [ ] Reaffirm interest and fit
- [ ] Follow up if no response in stated timeline
