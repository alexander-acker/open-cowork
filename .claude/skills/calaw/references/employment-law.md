# Employment Law and Workplace Rights Reference

> **DISCLAIMER**: This reference provides general educational information about US employment law, with emphasis on California. It does not constitute legal advice. Laws change frequently and vary by jurisdiction. Always consult a licensed employment attorney for specific legal questions.

## US Federal Employment Laws

### Key Statutes

| Law | Coverage | Key Protections |
|-----|----------|----------------|
| **Title VII (Civil Rights Act)** | 15+ employees | Prohibits discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex, national origin |
| **ADA (Americans with Disabilities Act)** | 15+ employees | Disability discrimination, reasonable accommodation |
| **ADEA (Age Discrimination)** | 20+ employees | Protects workers 40+ from age discrimination |
| **FLSA (Fair Labor Standards Act)** | Most employers | Minimum wage, overtime, child labor |
| **FMLA (Family Medical Leave Act)** | 50+ employees, 12+ months tenure | 12 weeks unpaid job-protected leave |
| **EPA (Equal Pay Act)** | All employers | Equal pay for equal work regardless of sex |
| **WARN Act** | 100+ employees | 60 days notice before mass layoffs |
| **OSHA** | Most employers | Workplace safety standards |
| **NLRA** | Private sector | Right to organize, collective bargaining |
| **USERRA** | All employers | Military service member reemployment rights |
| **PDA (Pregnancy Discrimination Act)** | 15+ employees | Prohibits pregnancy-based discrimination |
| **GINA** | 15+ employees | Prohibits genetic information discrimination |

### FLSA: Overtime and Classification

**Exempt vs. Non-Exempt**:
- **Non-exempt**: Entitled to overtime (1.5x after 40 hrs/week)
- **Exempt**: Must meet ALL three tests:
  1. Salary basis: Paid fixed salary regardless of hours
  2. Salary level: Minimum $844/week ($43,888/year) as of 2024
  3. Duties test: Executive, administrative, professional, computer, or outside sales

**Common Misclassification Issues**:
- Independent contractor vs. employee (ABC test in many states)
- Exempt vs. non-exempt (job title alone doesn't determine status)
- Intern vs. employee (primary beneficiary test)

### FMLA Quick Reference

**Eligibility**: 12+ months employed, 1,250+ hours worked, employer has 50+ employees within 75 miles

**Covered Reasons**:
- Birth/placement of child
- Serious health condition (self)
- Serious health condition (spouse, child, parent)
- Military qualifying exigency
- Military caregiver leave (26 weeks)

## California-Specific Employment Law

### California FEHA (Fair Employment and Housing Act)

**Broader than federal law** — covers employers with 5+ employees (1+ for harassment)

**Protected classes include all federal categories plus**:
- Sexual orientation and gender identity
- Marital status
- Military/veteran status
- Medical condition (cancer, genetic characteristics)
- Political activity or affiliation
- Status as victim of domestic violence, sexual assault, or stalking

### California Wage and Hour

**Key Differences from Federal Law**:

| Topic | Federal (FLSA) | California |
|-------|---------------|------------|
| Minimum wage (2024) | $7.25/hr | $16.00/hr (statewide, higher in some cities) |
| Overtime trigger | >40 hrs/week | >8 hrs/day OR >40 hrs/week |
| Double time | None | >12 hrs/day or >8 hrs on 7th consecutive day |
| Meal break | No requirement | 30 min if >5 hrs; 2nd if >10 hrs |
| Rest break | No requirement | 10 min per 4 hrs worked |
| Exempt salary threshold | $684/week | 2x state minimum wage (~$66,560/yr for 2024) |

**Meal Break Rules (California)**:
- Must be provided before end of 5th hour
- Must be 30 minutes and duty-free
- Employee must be relieved of all duties
- Penalty for violation: 1 hour premium pay per day

**Rest Break Rules (California)**:
- 10 minutes per 4 hours (or major fraction)
- Must be uninterrupted and duty-free
- Penalty for violation: 1 hour premium pay per day

### California Final Pay Requirements

| Separation Type | Deadline |
|----------------|----------|
| Fired/laid off | Immediately (same day) |
| Quit with 72+ hrs notice | Last day of work |
| Quit without notice | Within 72 hours |
| Seasonal/temporary worker | Within 24-72 hours |

**Penalty for late final pay**: Waiting time penalty of full day's wages for each day late (up to 30 days)

### California Non-Compete Law

As of January 1, 2024 (AB 1076 and SB 699):
- **Non-compete agreements are void and unenforceable** in California
- Applies regardless of where the agreement was signed
- Employers cannot even require employees to sign them
- Applies to former employees retroactively
- Employers must notify current/former employees that void NCAs are no longer enforceable

**Exceptions**:
- Sale of business (equity holders only)
- Trade secret protections still apply

### California PAGA (Private Attorneys General Act)

- Allows employees to sue on behalf of themselves AND other employees for Labor Code violations
- Acts as a private enforcement mechanism for labor laws
- 75% of penalties go to the state; 25% to aggrieved employees
- Must provide notice to LWDA before filing
- Powerful tool for wage and hour violations

## Common Workplace Issues

### Wrongful Termination
**"At-will" doesn't mean "for any reason"** — Illegal to terminate for:
- Discrimination based on protected class
- Retaliation for protected activity (whistleblowing, complaints, FMLA use)
- Violation of public policy
- Breach of implied contract (employee handbook promises)

### Workplace Harassment
**Legal standard**: Conduct must be severe OR pervasive enough to create a hostile work environment

**Employer liability**:
- Supervisor harassment: Employer strictly liable (CA)
- Co-worker harassment: Liable if knew/should have known and failed to act
- Third-party harassment: Liable if failed to take reasonable steps

### Retaliation
**Protected activities include**:
- Filing discrimination/harassment complaints
- Participating in investigations
- Requesting reasonable accommodations
- Taking FMLA/CFRA leave
- Reporting safety violations (OSHA)
- Whistleblowing on illegal activity
- Wage and hour complaints
- Filing workers' compensation claims

### Reasonable Accommodations (ADA/FEHA)
**Process**:
1. Employee discloses disability and need for accommodation
2. Employer engages in interactive process (good faith dialogue)
3. Employer provides reasonable accommodation unless undue hardship

**Common accommodations**: Modified schedule, telework, ergonomic equipment, job restructuring, reassignment, leave of absence

## Filing Complaints

### Federal
- **EEOC**: Discrimination/harassment (file within 180-300 days)
- **DOL**: Wage/hour violations
- **OSHA**: Safety complaints
- **NLRB**: Union/organizing issues

### California
- **DFEH/CRD**: Discrimination/harassment (file within 3 years)
- **DLSE**: Wage claims (3-4 year statute of limitations)
- **Cal/OSHA**: Safety complaints
- **LWDA**: PAGA claims (1 year statute of limitations)

## Statute of Limitations Quick Reference (California)

| Claim Type | Time Limit |
|-----------|-----------|
| FEHA discrimination/harassment | 3 years (to file with CRD) |
| Wrongful termination (tort) | 2 years |
| Breach of written contract | 4 years |
| Breach of oral contract | 2 years |
| Unpaid wages | 3 years (4 years under UCL) |
| PAGA claims | 1 year |
| FMLA interference/retaliation | 2 years (3 years if willful) |
| EEOC charge (federal) | 300 days (with state agency) |
