## Transition Planning Template

### Phase 1: Self-Assessment (Weeks 1-2)
1. **Skills inventory**: List all skills — technical, soft, domain-specific
2. **Values clarification**: What matters most? (impact, income, flexibility, growth)
3. **Interest mapping**: What activities energize you vs. drain you?
4. **Constraint analysis**: Financial runway, family obligations, location requirements

### Phase 2: Target Definition (Weeks 2-4)
1. **Research roles**: Read 20+ job descriptions in target field
2. **Informational interviews**: Talk to 5+ people in the target role
3. **Gap analysis**: Compare your skills inventory to job requirements
4. **Reality check**: Validate timeline, compensation expectations, entry points

### Phase 3: Skill Building (Months 1-6)
1. **Prioritize gaps**: Focus on the top 3 skills that appear in 80% of job postings
2. **Learning plan**: Courses, certifications, boot camps, self-study
3. **Portfolio building**: Create 2-3 projects demonstrating new skills
4. **Volunteer/freelance**: Gain real experience while still employed

### Phase 4: Positioning (Months 3-9)
1. **Resume rewrite**: Lead with transferable skills and bridge narrative
2. **LinkedIn optimization**: Headline and summary reflect target role
3. **Network building**: Attend industry events, join communities
4. **Narrative crafting**: Develop your "Why I'm making this change" story

### Phase 5: Execution (Months 6-12)
1. **Apply strategically**: Focus on roles that explicitly value diverse backgrounds
2. **Leverage warm introductions**: Referrals have 10x higher conversion
3. **Interview preparation**: Practice bridging current experience to target role
4. **Negotiate**: Don't undersell — your prior experience has value

## Career Change Narrative Framework

### The Bridge Story Structure
1. **Where I was**: Briefly describe previous career (credibility)
2. **The catalyst**: What sparked the desire to change (authenticity)
3. **The connection**: How your past directly prepares you for the new role (relevance)
4. **What I've done**: Concrete steps taken to prepare (commitment)
5. **Where I'm going**: Why this specific role/company (enthusiasm)

### Example Narrative
> "After 8 years in financial services, I developed deep expertise in data analysis, stakeholder management, and translating complex information for diverse audiences. Working with our analytics team on customer segmentation sparked my passion for product thinking. Over the past year, I completed a PM certification, led two internal product improvements, and built a prototype app. I'm excited about this role at [Company] because [specific reason tied to their product/mission]."

## Financial Planning for Career Transitions

### Minimum Runway Calculation
- **Essential monthly expenses** x 6 months = minimum cash reserve
- Add: Health insurance costs if losing employer coverage
- Add: Education/certification costs
- Add: 20% buffer for unexpected expenses

### Income Bridge Strategies
- Freelance/consulting in current field while transitioning
- Part-time work in target field for experience + income
- Negotiate extended notice period with current employer
- Use employer tuition reimbursement before leaving
